About the Glucose-Insulin Simulator

This interactive simulator was developed by [Your Name] for the University of Arkansas Biomedical Engineering Department (Spring 2025). It models plasma glucose and insulin dynamics using Bergman's Minimal Model, enhanced with adaptations for exercise and individual patient characteristics.

Key Features:

  • Implemented in Python and Flask for a responsive web interface
  • Numerical solution of differential equations using SciPy's solve_ivp
  • Data visualization with Matplotlib, dynamically rendered in the browser
  • User inputs: age, sex, weight, fasting glucose, insulin dose, exercise intensity, and duration
  • Compares glucose trajectories for multiple dosing scenarios

The core model parameters (p1, p2, p3) are drawn from Roy & Parker (2007), "Dynamic Modeling of Exercise Effects on Plasma Glucose and Insulin Levels," accounting for increased insulin clearance and glucose uptake during activity. Users can explore how physiological factors alter glucose regulation over a 6-hour period post-dose.
